Mental Health Support for Students

About the Project

Our team focuses on addressing stress and anxiety among students. Fast-paced academic schedules and life pressures often lead to burnout, impacting academic performance and well-being.

What is Stress?

Stress is the body's response to negative emotions or challenging situations. While some stress is normal, excessive stress can harm both mental and physical health.

Tips to Reduce Stress

  • Shift Your Focus: Engage in positive activities like meeting friends, watching a comforting movie, or exploring nature.
  • Stay Active: Exercise, walk, or do chores to release pent-up energy.
  • Breathing Exercises: Help regulate heart rate and reduce tension.
  • Explore Hobbies: Reading, crafting, or learning a new skill can boost your mood.
  • Interact with Pets: Spend time with animals to reduce stress.
  • Color Patterns: Try coloring detailed images to calm your mind.
  • Embrace Comfort: Hug loved ones to release positive chemicals in the brain.

Summary

Overcoming stress requires a comprehensive approach. Discovering personal strategies, incorporating sports, and improving sleep and relaxation can significantly improve mental health.
